Explain the difference between a medical condition and a related symptom.
Input
Condition: Parkinson's Disease Symptom: Tremor
Output
Parkinson's disease is a progressive neurological condition that affects the movement and coordination of an individual. It is characterized by the degeneration of specific nerve cells in the brain, resulting in a decreased production of the neurotransmitter dopamine. A tremor, on the other hand, is a symptom commonly associated with Parkinson's disease. It refers to the involuntary, rhythmic shaking or oscillating movement of a body part, usually the hands or fingers. Tremors can also be associated with other medical conditions or certain medications. While a symptom like tremor can be indicative of Parkinson's disease, it alone is not sufficient to diagnose the condition. A comprehensive evaluation by a neurologist, along with the presence of other symptoms and findings, is required for diagnosis.
