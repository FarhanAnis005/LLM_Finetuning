### Input:


### Response:
The drug interaction between Warfarin and Vitamin K-containing foods is concerning because Warfarin is an anticoagulant that helps prevent blood clots. However, Vitamin K can help your body produce clotting factors, potentially reducing the effectiveness of Warfarin. This can lead to an increased risk of bleeding. To manage this interaction, patients taking Warfarin should avoid consuming large amounts of Vitamin K-rich foods (such as leafy green vegetables) or take a consistent daily dose of Vitamin K supplements if advised by their healthcare provider. Regular monitoring of the INR (International Normalized Ratio) is also important to adjust Warfarin dosage accordingly.
